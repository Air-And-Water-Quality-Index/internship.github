Introduction

The quality of air and water plays a crucial role in maintaining a healthy environment and safeguarding public health. As pollution levels rise due to urbanization and industrial activities, it becomes essential to monitor and evaluate these resources regularly.

Air and Water Quality Index

AQI monitors pollutants like PM2.5, PM10, ozone, NO₂, SO₂, and CO. It categorizes air quality as Good (0–50), Moderate (51–100), or Unhealthy (101+).

WQI assesses pH, dissolved oxygen, turbidity, temperature, and biological content. It’s rated as Excellent (0–25), Good (26–50), or Poor (50+).
